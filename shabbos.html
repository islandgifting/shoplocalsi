When Does Shabbos End?

Shabbos ends at nightfall — tzeis hakochavim (“the emergence of the stars”). Communities follow different opinions on exactly when that moment is. The main three shitos followed in our community are below.

Earliest · ~40 min
—
R. Tukachinsky
Approximately 40 minutes after shkia. Followed in Eretz Yisrael and by many Sefardim.
3 Medium Stars · ~50 min
—
Common in the US
Approximately 50 minutes after shkia, when three medium-sized stars are visible. The most widely accepted zman in Ashkenazi communities.
Latest · 72 min
—
Rabbeinu Tam
72 minutes after shkia. Followed by many chassidishe communities and used as a chumra by others.
Ask your rav: which zman to follow is a halachic question with different accepted opinions. Speak with your local rav to know which shita to keep, especially for melacha, havdalah, and issurei d’oraisa.
