Rokud Krumpli

Eggs & Potatoes · A Popular Hungarian Dish

Think of this as the ultimate rustic potato lasagna. We layer tender, thinly sliced boiled potatoes with sliced hard-boiled eggs, coating each layer in melted butter and generous dollops of well-salted sour cream (tejföl). Baked until the edges get completely golden and crispy, it is an incredible crowd-pleaser.

Prep 30 mins
Cook 40 mins
Yields 6 Servings
Ingredients
  • 8 medium Yukon Gold or Russet potatoes
  • 8 large Eggs
  • 2 cups Full-fat sour cream
  • 6 tbsp Unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tsp Sweet Hungarian paprika
  • 1½ tsp Salt (divided)
  • ¼ tsp Black pepper
Instructions
  1. Prep the Foundations. Place potatoes (skins on) in a large pot of salted water. In a separate pot, place the eggs. Bring both to a boil. Cook the eggs for 11 minutes, then plunge into ice water. Cook the potatoes until fork-tender (about 20-25 minutes). Let both cool completely. Once cool, peel the potatoes and eggs, then slice them both into uniform ¼-inch rounds.
  2. Season the Sour Cream. In a medium bowl, mix the sour cream with 1 teaspoon of salt, black pepper, and a splash of milk if it's too thick to spread easily.
  3. Layer the Casserole. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13 glass baking dish with a tablespoon of the melted butter. Shingle half of the potato slices along the bottom. Season lightly with salt. Layer all of the sliced hard-boiled eggs directly over the potatoes. Drizzle with half of the remaining melted butter and dot with half of the sour cream mixture.
  4. Finish and Bake. Add the remaining potato slices on top. Spread the rest of the sour cream evenly across the top layer. Drizzle the last of the melted butter and dust the top with sweet Hungarian paprika for color. Bake uncovered for 35 to 40 minutes until bubbling and the edges turn a deep golden brown. Serve hot with a side of cold vinegar cucumber salad!

Grandma's Plum Jam Pockets

Lekváros Derelye

Pillowy, tender potato dough wrapped around a gooey, tart center of thick plum lekvar, all rolled in a crisp, buttery, cinnamon-sugar breadcrumb coating.

Prep 45 mins
Cook 15 mins
Yields 4-6 Servings
Ingredients
  • 2 lbs Russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups All-purpose flour
  • 1 large Egg
  • 2 tbsp Unsalted butter, melted & cooled
  • ½ tsp Salt
  • 1 cup Thick Hungarian plum lekvar (or prune)
  • 1½ cups Plain breadcrumbs
  • 6 tbsp Unsalted butter (for coating)
  • ⅓ cup Granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp Ground cinnamon
Instructions
  1. Mash and Cool the Potatoes. Boil cubed potatoes until fork-tender. Drain thoroughly. Mash them exceptionally well or press through a potato ricer until completely smooth. Spread the mashed potatoes across a baking sheet and let them cool completely to room temperature.
  2. Toast the Breadcrumbs. Melt 6 tablespoons of butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the breadcrumbs and cook, stirring constantly, for 3 to 5 minutes until deep golden brown. Remove from heat, stir in the sugar and cinnamon, and set aside.
  3. Form the Dough. In a large bowl, combine the cooled potatoes, flour, egg, 2 tablespoons of melted butter, and salt. Knead gently with your hands right in the bowl just until a soft dough forms. Do not overwork it.
  4. Roll and Fill. Bring a large pot of water to a gentle boil. On a well-floured surface, roll the dough to a ¼-inch thickness. Visually divide the dough in half. On one half, place 1-teaspoon dollops of plum lekvar in a grid, spaced 2 inches apart. Fold the empty half of the dough cleanly over the fillings.
  5. Cut and Cook. Press down gently around each pocket of jam to seal. Cut into square pockets using a knife or pastry wheel. Crimp the edges with a fork. Drop into the boiling water in batches. Once they float to the top, boil for 2 more minutes, then lift out with a slotted spoon and toss directly into the warm breadcrumb skillet until fully coated.

Hungarian Cheese Noodles

Túrós Csusza

The ultimate old-world cousin to Mac & Cheese. It takes under 15 minutes from box to table, uses simple pantry staples, and perfectly satisfies that savory noodle craving.

Prep 5 mins
Cook 10 mins
Yields 4 Servings
Ingredients
  • 1 lb Wide egg noodles (or square pasta flakes)
  • 4 tbsp Unsalted butter
  • 1½ cups Curd-style cottage cheese or farmer's cheese (túró)
  • 1 cup Full-fat sour cream
  • ½ tsp Salt
  • ¼ tsp Black pepper
Instructions
  1. Boil the Pasta. Cook the egg noodles in a large pot of heavily salted boiling water according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. Melt and Coat. Drain the noodles thoroughly, but do not rinse them. Return them immediately to the warm pot off the heat and add the butter, tossing until completely melted and the noodles are glossy.
  3. Fold the Dairy. Crumble the cottage cheese or farmer's cheese directly into the warm noodles. Add the sour cream, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Heat Gently. Turn the burner onto ultra-low heat and toss gently for 1 to 2 minutes just until the sour cream is warm and creating a velvety coating, and the pockets of cheese are deeply incorporated. Serve immediately in wide bowls.

Sweet Cheese Dumplings

Túrógombóc

Tennis-ball-sized dumplings made from a dense mixture of farmer's cheese and semolina. They are boiled until plump, rolled in golden toasted breadcrumbs, and served warm. Eating just two of these constitutes a massive, incredibly satisfying meal.

Prep 15 mins + 1h chill
Cook 15 mins
Yields 4 Servings (8 large dumplings)
Ingredients
  • 1 lb Dry farmer's cheese or ricotta (drained well)
  • 4 large Eggs, separated
  • 1 cup Cream of Wheat (Semolina / Gríz)
  • 4 tbsp Unsalted butter
  • 1 cup Plain breadcrumbs
  • 2 tbsp Granulated sugar
  • For serving: Sour cream and powdered sugar
Instructions
  1. Mix the Cheese Base. In a large bowl, combine the farmer's cheese, egg yolks, and semolina. Mix well until smooth. Whip the egg whites separately until soft peaks form, then fold them gently into the cheese mixture.
  2. Chill. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour so the semolina fully absorbs the moisture and the dough firms up.
  3. Toast the Breadcrumbs. Melt the butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the breadcrumbs and cook, stirring constantly, for 3-5 minutes until deep golden brown. Stir in the sugar and set aside.
  4. Shape and Boil. Bring a large pot of water to a gentle boil. With wet hands, form the cheese mixture into 8 large tennis-ball-sized dumplings. Gently drop into the boiling water. Once they float, cook another 10 minutes.
  5. Coat and Serve. Lift dumplings out with a slotted spoon and roll immediately in the warm breadcrumbs. Serve warm topped with a big dollop of sour cream and a dusting of powdered sugar.

Caramelized Cabbage Noodles

Kaposztás Tészta · Seudah Mafseket

The ultimate low-sodium, carb-heavy Hungarian pasta to fuel the 25-hour fast without causing thirst. Deeply caramelized cabbage tossed with egg noodles is a classic pre-fast meal.

Prep 20 mins
Cook 40 mins
Yields 4-6 Servings
Ingredients
  • 1 medium Green cabbage (about 2 lbs)
  • 1 lb Wide egg noodles
  • 4 tbsp Neutral oil or butter
  • 2 tbsp Sugar
  • 1 tsp Salt (for salting cabbage)
  • ¼ tsp Black pepper
Instructions
  1. Shred the Cabbage. Remove the core from the cabbage. Grate the cabbage using the coarse holes of a box grater, or shred it exceptionally fine using a food processor. Place the shredded cabbage in a colander, toss with 1 teaspoon of salt, and let it sit for 15 minutes to draw out excess water. Squeeze the cabbage firmly to remove as much liquid as possible.
  2. Slow-Caramelize the Base. In a large deep skillet or Dutch oven, heat the oil or butter over medium heat. Add the sugar and let it melt for 1 minute. Add the squeezed cabbage and stir well to coat. Turn heat down to medium-low. Cook uncovered for 30 to 35 minutes, stirring every few minutes. The cabbage will dramatically shrink and turn a deep, beautiful caramel-gold color.
  3. Cook the Noodles. While the cabbage finishes caramelizing, bring a large pot of water to a boil and cook your egg noodles until perfectly al dente. Drain well, but do not rinse.
  4. Toss and Serve. Dump the hot, drained noodles directly into the skillet with the caramelized cabbage. Add the black pepper and toss vigorously over low heat for 2 minutes until the noodles are fully integrated into the sweet cabbage paste. Serve warm as a comforting, gentle pre-fast fuel.

Creamy Hungarian Mushroom Soup

Gombaleves

A rich, velvety soup with an intense earthy depth. Spiced heavily with sweet Hungarian paprika, freshened up with plenty of dill, and made creamy with sour cream, it's the ultimate comforting starter.

Prep 15 mins
Cook 25 mins
Yields 4-6 Servings
Ingredients
  • 1 lb Fresh mushrooms (cremini or white), sliced
  • 1 medium Yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 4 tbsp Unsalted butter
  • 3 tbsp All-purpose flour
  • 2 cups Vegetable broth (or water)
  • 1 cup Whole milk
  • ½ cup Full-fat sour cream
  • 1 tbsp Sweet Hungarian paprika
  • 2 tbsp Fresh dill, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp Lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • ¼ tsp Black pepper
Instructions
  1. Sauté the Aromatics. Melt the butter in a large heavy pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for 5 minutes until soft and translucent. Add the sliced mushrooms and sauté for another 5 minutes until they release their liquid and start to shrink.
  2. Create the Paprika Base. Turn the heat down to low. Sprinkle the flour and sweet Hungarian paprika directly over the mushrooms and onions. Stir constantly for 1 minute to coat everything evenly. Be careful not to let the paprika burn, or it will turn bitter.
  3. Simmer & Build. Slowly pour in the vegetable broth and whole milk, whisking constantly to smooth out any flour lumps. Bring the soup to a gentle boil over medium heat, then reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 15 minutes until slightly thickened.
  4. Temper the Sour Cream. Remove the pot from the heat. In a small bowl, whisk the sour cream with a ladleful of the hot soup broth to temper it (this stops it from curdling). Stir the tempered sour cream, fresh dill, lemon juice, salt, and black pepper into the pot until perfectly smooth. Serve warm!

Cold Sour Cherry Soup

Hideg Meggyleves

A legendary Hungarian summer phenomenon. Sweet, tart, creamy, spiced with cinnamon, and served ice-cold. Don't think of it as dessert — in July, Bubbies serve this right at the start of the meal to cool everyone down!

Prep 10 mins
Cook 15 mins + 3-4h chill
Yields 4 Servings
Ingredients
  • 2 jars (24 oz each) Kosher pitted sour cherries in light syrup (look for imported glass jars of Hungarian or Polish sour cherries)
  • 1 cup Water
  • ½ cup Sugar (adjust down if the cherry syrup is very sweet)
  • 1 stick Cinnamon
  • 3 whole Cloves
  • 1 cup Sour cream
  • 2 tbsp All-purpose flour
  • A pinch Of salt
Instructions
  1. Simmer the Cherries and Spices. Empty both jars of sour cherries, including all of their liquid syrup, into a large pot. Add the water, sugar, cinnamon stick, cloves, and a tiny pinch of salt. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, then lower the heat and let it simmer gently for 10 minutes until the cherries are soft.
  2. Build the Creamy Thickener. While the soup simmers, whisk the sour cream and flour together in a medium bowl until a smooth paste forms with no lumps.
  3. Thicken and Temper. Ladle about 1 cup of the hot cherry liquid from the pot into your sour cream mixture, whisking rapidly to combine and warm it up. Slowly pour this tempered mixture back into the boiling pot, stirring constantly. Let the soup bubble gently for 2 minutes until it thickens into a silky, rich consistency.
  4. Fish Out Spices and Ice It Down. Turn off the heat. Use a spoon to fish out and discard the cinnamon stick and whole cloves. Let the soup cool to room temperature, then transfer to a container and refrigerate for at least 3 to 4 hours until completely ice-cold. Serve in chilled bowls, topped with an extra tiny dollop of sour cream or a leaf of fresh mint!
Small tricks, big difference

Kitchen & Home Tips

Little hacks passed down from Bubbies, neighbors, and years of trial & error. Send us yours — we'll add them to the collection.

🕯️
Shabbos prep

Cool soup faster before Shabbos

Pour hot chicken soup into a wide 9x13 pan instead of a tall pot. Cools in 30 minutes instead of 3 hours — and it's safer to move to the fridge before Shabbos.

🧂
Bakery hack

Rescue oversalted soup

Peel a raw potato, cut in half, and drop into the pot. Simmer 15 minutes, then remove. The potato absorbs excess salt.

🥶
Freezer wisdom

Freeze challah dough, not baked

Shape and freeze raw challah on a tray. Transfer to bag once frozen. Take out Thursday morning to thaw and rise — you get bakery-fresh challah every week.

🍅
Meal prep

The Sunday "protein prep"

Every Sunday, bake 3 chickens, hard-boil a dozen eggs, and make a huge pot of rice. You have easy lunches and quick suppers all week without cooking dinner from scratch.

✨
Erev Shabbos

The 15-minute kitchen reset

Set a timer for 15 minutes right after licht bentchen prep. Wipe counters, load dishwasher, sweep floor. It doesn't have to be perfect — just done. Shabbos feels 10x calmer.

🍋
Bakery hack

Extend your fresh herbs

Wrap fresh parsley or dill in a slightly damp paper towel, then put inside a plastic bag in the fridge. Stays fresh 2 weeks instead of 3 days.

🧽
Home & hearth

Baking soda + vinegar for the sink

Weekly: pour ½ cup baking soda down the drain, then 1 cup white vinegar. Wait 15 min, flush with hot water. Prevents clogs, kills smell.

👶
Mother's helper

The "5-minute pickup rule"

Every night before bed: everyone in the house picks up for 5 minutes only. Kids too. In a week you save hours of deep-cleaning — and teach kids the habit.

For the little ones

Kids' Corner — No Electronics Needed

Ideas to save your sanity on long summer afternoons and hot Shabbos weekends. Grouped by situation — pick a tab, pick an idea, and go.

All ages

Parsha Charades

Pick a scene from the parsha. Act it out silently. Family guesses. Best for Yaakov and the sheep, Yosef and the dreams, the makos.

Ages 5+

Story chain

One person says a sentence to start a story. Next person adds one sentence. Keep going around the table — the sillier it gets, the better.

Ages 4+

Alef-bais scavenger hunt

Go through the house pointing to objects in Hebrew alphabet order. Kids compete to be first. Bonus: make a written list.

All ages

Guess who

One person thinks of a person from Chumash or a family member. Others ask yes/no questions to guess who. First to guess wins.

Ages 6+

Card castles

Deck of cards + hours of quiet focus. Whoever builds the tallest before it falls wins. Great for competitive kids.

Ages 3+

Family show & tell

Each kid picks a favorite item and does a mini "presentation" — where it came from, why they love it. Toddlers eat it up.

All ages

Backyard ice hunt

Freeze small toys in ice cube trays. Kids "excavate" them with spoons and warm water. Cools them down and buys you 45 minutes.

Ages 3+

Sidewalk chalk mural

Give each kid a section of the sidewalk. When done, walk the whole family through the "art gallery" and give awards.

Ages 5+

Lemonade stand

Set up out front. Real money, real customers. Kids learn making change, customer service, and marketing. Great mitzvah opportunity: donate to tzedakah.

All ages

The nature bingo card

Make a bingo card of things to find outside: red leaf, feather, three types of rocks, a bug, a puddle. Walk the block. First to bingo wins.

Ages 4+

Backyard obstacle course

Chairs, cushions, hula hoops, a broomstick. Have older kids design it. Time each other. Bonus: video the runs.

All ages

Sprinkler paintbrush

Give kids paintbrushes and buckets of water. Let them "paint" the fence, the driveway, the siding. Water evaporates — endless canvas.

Ages 4+

Sock puppet theater

Old socks + markers + buttons + yarn. Kids make puppets, then perform a show for the family after supper. Curtains: a blanket over two chairs.

Ages 3+

Restaurant at home

Kids write "menus," set the table, take orders. Serve real snacks. Amazing pretend-play. Great vocabulary building.

All ages

Fort under the dining table

Sheets, chairs, cushions, flashlights. Read books inside. Serve lunch in the fort as a special treat. Instant magic.

Ages 5+

Cardboard box marble maze

Cut open a cereal box or shoe box. Kids glue in cardboard pieces and straws to make a marble maze. Design + engineering + hours of play.

Ages 3+

Sensory bin

Big plastic tub + rice or dry beans + small toys hidden inside + scoops and cups. Kids can play for an hour. Contain to one room!

Ages 6+

Kid Iron Chef

Give kids 3 safe ingredients from the pantry. They "compete" to make a snack. You judge. Winner gets to pick supper for that night.

Ages 1-3

Muffin tin sort

Give a muffin tin and a bowl of pom-poms, cheerios, or small blocks. Toddler "sorts" by color into the tin. 30 quiet minutes.

Ages 2-4

Water pouring station

Two cups, one bowl of water, tray underneath. Toddler pours water back and forth. Improves fine motor. Bring towels.

Ages 1-3

Sticker page

Sheet of stickers + blank paper. Toddlers can peel and stick for a shockingly long time. Buy stickers in bulk on Amazon.

Ages 2+

Play-dough tray

Homemade play-dough (flour, salt, water, food color) + cookie cutters + a rolling pin. Set them up at the table. Save the tray for next time.

Ages 1-2

Basket of "safe" kitchen tools

Wooden spoons, plastic measuring cups, silicone spatulas, small pot. Give them their own drawer in the kitchen. Endless fascination.

Ages 2-4

Painter's tape road map

Painter's tape on the floor becomes roads. Toy cars zoom around. Easy to peel up after. Endless configurations.

The Power of "I Noticed"

Instead of praising with "Good job!" try starting with "I noticed…" — "I noticed how patiently you waited," "I noticed you helped your sister without being asked." Specific praise builds specific character. Kids remember what you saw, not just that you approved.
